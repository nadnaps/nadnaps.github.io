Cambridge, MA, USA

In August this year, I will be starting my post-doctoral research with the support of the George F. Carrier fellowship in the Applied Mathematics department at Harvard University in USA.

In July 2017, I graduated with a PhD under the supervision of Prof. Roberto Verzicco and Prof. Detlef Lohse for my thesis titled ‘Bubbles and drops in turbulent Taylor-Couette flow: numerical modelling and simulation’. Following my PhD, I continued in the same group as part of short post-doctoral stint.

The main focus of my thesis was to understand the dynamics of bubbles and drops in a wall-bounded turbulent flows. I am also interested in the field of fluid-structure interaction and in the development of new techniques and models for understanding multi-physics flows.
