Vamsi Spandan

I am currently the George Carrier Fellow in the Applied Maths department at Harvard University in Cambridge, USA.

The main theme of my research is Computational Science and I focus on the following topics.

  • Fluid Mechanics, Turbulence and Multiphase Flows
  • Biophysics, Neuroscience and Biomedical Engineering
  • Reinforcement Learning and Deep Learning in Fluid Mechanics
  • Computational Physics and High Performance Computing

I am broadly interested in Multi-physics systems, i.e. systems involving multiple simultaneously occurring physical processes.

I received my PhD in Applied Physics at the Physics of Fluids and Max-Planck Center for Complex Fluid Dynamics, Netherlands. Prior to my doctoral research, I graduated with a Bachelor and Masters of Technology in Mechanical Engineering from Indian Institute of Technology, Madras.

My doctoral thesis is titled ‘Bubbles and drops in turbulent Taylor-Couette flow: numerical modelling and simulation’. The main focus of the thesis was to understand the dynamics of bubbles and drops in turbulent flows. The thesis also contains topics in the field of fluid-structure interaction and the development of new computational techniques and algorithms for understanding multi-physics flows.
