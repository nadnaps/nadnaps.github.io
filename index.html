I am currently the George Carrier Fellow in the Applied Maths department at Harvard University in Cambridge, USA.

My primary research interests are in Computational Science and Multiphysics systems with a focus on fluid flows.

In July 2017, I defended my Phd thesis titled ‘Bubbles and drops in turbulent Taylor-Couette flow: numerical modelling and simulation’ at the Physics of Fluids and Max-Planck Center for Complex Fluid Dynamics, Netherlands.

The main focus of the thesis was to understand the dynamics of bubbles and drops in a wall-bounded turbulent flows. I have also worked in the field of fluid-structure interaction and in the development of new computational techniques and models for understanding multi-physics flows.

In May 2013, I graduated with a Bachelor and Masters of Technology in Mechanical Engineering from Indian Institute of Technology, Madras.
