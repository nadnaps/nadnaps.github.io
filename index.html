I am currently the George Carrier Fellow in the Applied Maths department at Harvard University in Cambridge, USA. Over the years I have worked on projects spanning a wide range of disciplines, viz. Fluid Mechanics, Computational Physics, Biophysics, Reinforcement Learning in Physics, High Performance Computing and Data Visualisation.

I am broadly interested in Multi-physics systems, i.e. systems involving multiple simultaneously occurring physical processes. I am currently working on theoretical and computational problems in Biophysics and Neuroscience.

In July 2017, I defended my PhD thesis titled ‘Bubbles and drops in turbulent Taylor-Couette flow: numerical modelling and simulation’ at the Physics of Fluids and Max-Planck Center for Complex Fluid Dynamics, Netherlands.

The main focus of the thesis was to understand the dynamics of bubbles and drops in turbulent flows. The thesis also contains topics in the field of fluid-structure interaction and the development of new computational techniques and models for understanding multi-physics flows.

In May 2013, I graduated with a Bachelor and Masters of Technology in Mechanical Engineering from Indian Institute of Technology, Madras.
